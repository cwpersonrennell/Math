    Given that the circle has a radius of 9, express \(a\) (the length highlighted in red) as a function of \(\theta\).

    \[ a(\theta)= \]

    SolutionThe key to answering this question is to label the radius that is adjoined to \(a\). By doing so, it becomes clear that the length of the hypotenuse is \(9+a\). Thus, we can use the Sine function to get: \[ \solve{ \sin\theta &=&\dfrac{9}{9+a}\\ \left(9+a\right)\sin\theta &=&9\\ 9\sin\theta+a\sin\theta &=&9\\ a\sin\theta &=&9-9\sin\theta\\ a(\theta)&=&\dfrac{9-9\sin\theta}{\sin\theta} } \]
